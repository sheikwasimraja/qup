Why We Need A Tracker

One of the reasons we need a tracker is because every one of us should have the intention of understanding the Qur'an and growing closer to Allah through knowledge. Even if someone dies while they are in the path of seeking knowledge, they can still receive the reward for their intention and effort.

"Whoever travels a path in search of knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise."

Sahih Muslim 2699a / 2699

"Actions are only by intentions, and every person will have only what they intended."

Sahih al-Bukhari 1, 54, 6689 and Sahih Muslim 1907

"Whoever intends to do a good deed but does not do it, Allah records it with Himself as a complete good deed."

Sahih al-Bukhari 6491 and Sahih Muslim 131 / 128

"If My servant intends to do a good deed but does not do it, then write it as one good deed for him."

Sahih al-Bukhari 7501

So if a person sincerely intends to understand the Qur'an, learn Arabic, improve Salah, or seek knowledge for the sake of Allah, and they pass away before completing that journey, we hope that Allah will still give them the full reward because Allah rewards sincerity, intention, and effort.

1. Allah Sees The Striving

وَأَنْ لَيْسَ لِلْإِنسَانِ إِلَّا مَا سَعَىٰ

"And that man will have nothing except what he strives for." (53:39)

Allah sees the striving, the effort, and the sincere intention, even before the result comes.

2. Arabic Has Special Honor

Allah chose Arabic for the Qur'an out of all languages. That itself shows the importance and honor of Arabic.

إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تَعْقِلُونَ

"Indeed, We have sent it down as an Arabic Qur'an so that you may understand." (12:2)

3. The Qur'an Came In Clear Arabic

بِلِسَانٍ عَرَبِيٍّ مُبِينٍ

"In a clear Arabic language." (26:195)

إِنَّا جَعَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تَعْقِلُونَ

"Indeed, We have made it an Arabic Qur'an so that you may understand." (43:3)

4. Translation Helps, But Arabic Gives More

We can understand the meaning of the Qur'an through English translations, and that is beneficial. But translation is never exactly the same as the original Arabic.

It is like the difference between eating a fruit fresh from the tree and drinking processed juice. The juice gives you something, but the original fruit gives you the full taste and experience.

That is why we should all try to learn at least some Arabic.

Why This Matters In Salah

Every day in Salah, the Imam recites Qur'an in Arabic. If we do not understand what is being recited, our mind may wander to other things. But when we understand even a little of what is being said, our Salah becomes deeper, more focused, and more meaningful.

So Every Muslim Should Intend To

  • Understand the Qur'an.
  • Learn the meanings of what is recited in Salah.
  • Learn at least basic Arabic.
  • Grow closer to Allah through knowledge.
  • Keep track of progress so that learning stays consistent.

Even small steps matter when they are done sincerely for Allah.
